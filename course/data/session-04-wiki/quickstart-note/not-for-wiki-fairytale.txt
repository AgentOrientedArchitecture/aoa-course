The Lantern Baker

Once upon a time, in a village beside a silver river, a baker named Mira made
small honey cakes for the evening market. Every night she lit a blue lantern in
her shop window so travellers could find warm bread before the road grew dark.

One winter, the lantern went missing. Mira searched the mill, the bridge, and
the orchard, but found only footprints in the snow. At dawn, a child returned
the lantern and explained that he had borrowed it to guide his grandmother home
through the storm.

Mira laughed, shared the last honey cake, and hung the lantern even higher.
From then on, anyone in the village could borrow it when the path was hard to
see.
